How does Blockchain work




Abstract

To put it simply, blockchain is a distributed database that records digital transactions.
The blockchain is a distributed ledger in which each user's transaction history is recorded in
chronological order and could be altered by a consensus of all users



  • Blockchain does not require a central party to control it. The network controls itself.
  • Key applications include: financial transactions, supply chain tracking, stock market trading and elections.
  • The very first block of a blockchain is called a "genesis block".
  • The data of a blockchain is always up to date, so there are no outdated data records.
  • The most common blockchain programming languages are C++ and Java.
